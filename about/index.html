Who is Daniel Lombraña González?

During my PhD I discovered that my real passion is to research and build tools that could help other people to solve their problems (I guess, that's my engineering side speaking).

Right now I'm focused on my company Scifabric, where we develop easy-to-use technologies to collect, analyse and enrich (your) data with the crowd. We do this via our open source technology PYBOSSA and its hosted version Crowdcrafting.

PYBOSSA is the ultimate crowdsourcing framework to analyze or enrich data that can't be processed by machines alone. The technology has been used to stop Malaria in Africa, to fight cancer, to analyze oil contracts between Governments and major companies, to recover the Bronze Age collection of United Kingdom, to study antimatter, ...

PYBOSSA has been used by United Nations, CERN, British Museum, British Library, The Guardian, University College of London, to name just a few.

Crowdcrafting is a our PYBOSSA server that invites volunteers to contribute to scientific projects developed by citizens, professionals or institutions.
