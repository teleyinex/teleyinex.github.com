Who is Daniel Lombraña González?

During my PhD I discovered that my real passion is to research and build tools that could help other people to solve their problems (I guess, that's my engineering side speaking).

Right now I'm focused on building the citizen science framework PyBossa and its hosted version Crowdcrafting, where professional and amateur scientist create their research projects and invite people to help them to solve a problem.

Crowdcrafting has thousands of volunteers contributing to scientific projects like Feynman's Flowers where you can help analyzing nano particles, or Rural Geolocator where you can fight Malaria by geo-referencing houses to help in the logistics of deploying a new solar trap for mosquitoes. If you like the space, take a look at Dark Skies project where you can see what the astronauts of the International Space Station watch every day and fight light pollution.
